## 4. BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEW RESOURCES

### STAR Method
Format for behavioral answers:
- **S**ituation: Context of the problem
- **T**ask: What you owned
- **A**ction: Specific actions you took
- **R**esult: Outcome and impact

### Template Answer Structure
1. Set scene (20-30 seconds)
2. Your actions (60-90 seconds)
3. The result (20-30 seconds)
4. What you learned (10-20 seconds)

### Common Behavioral Questions by Company

**Amazon:**
- Tell about a time you failed
- Give an example of showing bias for action
- Where did you invent and simplify something?
- Tell us about leadership experience

**Google:**
- Describe a challenging project
- How do you handle conflicts?
- Tell about a time you learned something new
- How do you approach ambiguous problems?

**Meta:**
- Experience with fast-moving environment?
- When did you simplify/improve something?
- How do you handle pressure?
- Leadership and mentorship example?

**Microsoft:**
- How do you learn new technologies?
- Experience with large-scale projects?
- Cross-functional teamwork example?
- Handling failure and feedback?

### Preparation Tips
1. Prepare 3-5 strong stories
2. Each story should show different competencies
3. Use quantifiable results (10x faster, $1M saved, etc.)
4. Practice 2-minute delivery time
5. Record yourself and critique

  - Salary data

## 5. RESUME OPTIMIZATION

### Before Applying
- **ATS Optimization**: Use correct keywords
- **Metrics Matter**: "Built feature used by 5M users" > "Built feature"
- **Relevance**: Tailor to job description
- **Format**: Clear sections, consistent formatting

### Projects to Highlight
- **Impact Projects**: Show what you built and impact
- **Relevant Tech Stack**: Matches job requirement
- **Challenging Problems Solved**: Demonstrates thinking
- **Large-scale Systems** (L4+): Distributed, millions of users, etc.

### Common Resume Mistakes
- Too verbose (keep it concise)
- Generic descriptions ("worked on project")
- No metrics (always quantify)
- Wrong format/layout (ATS hostile)
- Irrelevant projects

### Resume Tools
- **Resumeworded**: Scores your resume against job
- **JobScan**: ATS keyword optimization
- **Re.Resume**: AI-powered review
- **Grammarly**: Grammar and clarity

## 6. INTERVIEW DAY TIPS

### Before the Interview
1. **Research the company**: Know 2-3 recent products/news
2. **Know your interviewer**: LinkedIn quick check (if visible)
3. **Prepare story**: 1-2 minute intro about yourself
4. **Technical setup**: Proper equipment, internet, quiet place
5. **Environment**: Water, restroom, comfort
6. **Calm nerves**: 5-10 mins breathing exercise

### During the Interview
1. **Listen carefully**: Understand problem fully before coding
2. **Think aloud**: Explain your approach before writing code
3. **Ask questions**: Clarifications, constraints, examples
4. **Start with examples**: Work through 1-2 examples manually
5. **Code clearly**: Good naming, comments, organized
6. **Test your code**: Walk through with examples
7. **Discuss trade-offs**: Time vs space, readability vs efficiency
8. **Show your work**: Don't just write code silently

### Communication Tips
- Speak clearly, not too fast
- Pause for interviewer feedback
- Ask "Is this approach good?" or "Should I optimize?"
- Acknowledge edge cases you spot
- Explain *why* you chose an algorithm, not just what

### After the Interview
1. **Thank you note**: Send within 24 hours (connects with recruiter)
2. **Mention highlights**: Key topics discussed, your highlights
3. **Demonstrate enthusiasm**: "Very interested in this role"
4. **Be professional but warm**
5. **Wait for feedback**: Don't follow up too early

## 10. FINAL CHECKLIST (Week Before Interview)

### One Week Before
- [ ] Review your top 20 problems (algorithms you might use)
- [ ] Practice 1-2 mock interviews
- [ ] Prepare stories for behavioral questions
- [ ] Research company thoroughly
- [ ] Check technical setup (webcam, microphone, internet)
- [ ] Review latest resume version
- [ ] Practice your introduction (2 mins)
- [ ] Get good sleep, reduce stress

### Day Before
- [ ] Light review of main algorithms
- [ ] Calm down, meditate
- [ ] Sleep 8 hours
- [ ] Prepare environment (quiet, clean)
- [ ] Arrive at location early (if in-person)

### Morning Of
- [ ] Eat healthy breakfast
- [ ] Drink water, avoid heavy caffeine
- [ ] Review top problems one more time
- [ ] Mental preparation (positive self-talk)
- [ ] Join call 5-10 mins early
- [ ] Deep breathing exercise

### Interview Approach
1. Listen to full problem statement
2. Ask clarifying questions
3. Think aloud about approach
4. Write clean code
5. Test with examples
6. Discuss optimization
7. Be confident but humble
